The Klein bottle is a non-orientable surface, meaning it impossible to distinguish between its *inside* or *outside* (see Figure 1). It is closely related to the well-known Möbius strip in that a Klein bottle can be produced by gluing two Möbius strips together along their boundaries. However, a Klein bottle is a 2D surface that can only be embedded 4D space, lest the surface self-intersects (see this [plus maths article](https://plus.maths.org/imaging-maths-inside-klein-bottle) for further illustrations and explanations).
